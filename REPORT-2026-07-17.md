# 🌲 Sherwood Chain [4663] Topology | Block: #12,191,237

> **Network State Summary:** Gross on-chain volume is dominated by a single mint-to-relay pipeline: a lone `0x0000...0000` minter feeds a two-hop conduit chain that alone carries roughly half of all USD flow, and every actively-priced asset reports a circularity ratio above 0.80 — meaning the corpus is overwhelmingly synthetic, closed-loop movement rather than organic position change. Real capital is concentrating in only a handful of nodes (SGOV, BABA, USO, QQQ), where net flow still runs positive despite the wash. The rest of the 98-symbol universe is either dormant, unpriced, or occupied by round-number ticker-squatters (ROBIN, PEPEROBIN, a 1-billion-supply NVDA clone).

## 🚨 4. Systemic Anomalies & Structural Warnings

*   **Amount Quantization:** Active nodes carry non-trivial precomputed `amountQuantization` (SGOV ~0.23, i.e. ~23 percent of transfers land on repeated rounded quantities). Combined with `interArrivalCV` above 4.0 on SGOV, the flow is bursty and script-timed rather than human-paced. The mint pipeline's 793 near-identical-value forwards across a single 1-to-1 relay is the clearest algorithmic-script fingerprint in the corpus.
*   **Contract-Labeling Bugs:** Three round-supply squatters are mislabeled as tradeable RWA: a **1,000,000,000**-supply NVDA clone (no price, colliding with the legit NVDA wrapper), **ROBIN** at **100,000,000,000** supply, and **PEPEROBIN** at **99,927,720** — all with no `exchange_rate`. **BE (Bloom Energy)** carries 6,353 holders and live transfers but a null price, so its state (large distribution) contradicts its metadata (unpriced/unvaluable).
*   **Illiquidity Squeeze / Velocity Warnings:** **`BABA`** turns over $1,543,063 of recent volume against a wrapper mcap of only **$177,943** — an **8.7x** volume-to-mcap ratio, meaning a tiny on-chain float is being cycled repeatedly (thin, manipulation-prone). **`NFLX`** shows the same shape: $747,447 volume on a $24,862 mcap (**30x** turnover) with circularity 0.97 — almost the entire float round-trips continuously. These are the highest squeeze-risk nodes on the chain.

## 🏹 5. Tactical On-Chain Playbooks & Diagnostics

### 🟩 Practical On-Chain Playbooks

*   **Play 1:** Blacklist the relay set — `0xcfAEce`, `0x1A18a8`, `0xeF5ca6`, `0xf4da3c` — and the two breadth hubs `0x006102b` and `0x6d56Ab` from any volume ranking. Removing them strips roughly $3.5M of pipeline gross (~half the corpus) and leaves a clean read of organic demand.
*   **Play 2:** For genuine multi-counterparty flow, focus only on **SGOV, QQQ, USO, and BABA** — they are the sole priced nodes combining meaningful net flow with more than a handful of counterparties. Treat **NFLX, SLV, CRCL** as untradeable wash loops (circularity greater than 0.95).
*   **Play 3:** Track the two true accumulators `0x9c856e` (+$434K) and `0x2F4579` (+$272K) as smart-money proxies — they are the only high-value nodes retaining, not forwarding, inflow.

### 🔧 MCP Server & Ingest Remediation List

1.  **Backfill missing `exchange_rate`.** BE, ROBIN, PEPEROBIN, the NVDA clone, SKHY, and WEEK all lack price, forcing USD flow to Not Computable for their entire holder bases (BE alone is 6,353 holders). Add a fallback oracle or explicitly tag `unpriced=true` so downstream tooling stops silently dropping them.
2.  **Namespace tickers by contract address, not symbol.** The 1B-supply NVDA clone colliding with the real NVDA wrapper corrupts every symbol-grouped aggregate. Key assets on `address` and expose a `isCanonical` / `isSquat` flag.
3.  **Add swap / DEX event classification.** The schema has no trade type — all flow is raw `Transfer`, so wash detection leans entirely on circularity heuristics. Ingesting AMM swap logs (and LP/TVL) would let subsequent reports compute real execution price and premium/discount versus the mirrored TradFi rate.
4.  **Tag known plumbing addresses canonically.** The mint source and the `0xcfAEce → 0x1A18a8` relay chain are stable across snapshots; a maintained `entityRole` label (mint / conduit / hub / sink) would let the MCP pre-filter synthetic volume server-side instead of every consumer re-deriving pass-through ratios by hand.
