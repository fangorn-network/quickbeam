## 🏹 5. Tactical On-Chain Playbooks & Diagnostics

### 🟩 Practical On-Chain Playbooks

*   **Play 1 — Blacklist the pipeline before ranking anything.** Exclude `0x0000…0000`, `0xcfAEce21…60A94`, `0x1A18a8b9…FAA4E7`, `0xfac1d7dC…190F16`, `0x6d56Ab47…Ba158`, `0xa8553DB0…E3C74`, `0xbeD9Ae2C…5C85F`. These seven addresses generate **68.3%** of gross sender-side USD volume and retain almost none of it. Any volume or whale ranking that includes them is measuring plumbing.
*   **Play 2 — Trade only where breadth survives audit.** **SGOV** is the sole node with turnover below float (0.37×), 86 holders, and no loop signature — the only instrument here behaving like an instrument. **USO** and **BABA** carry real size ($868k / $471k) with genuine counterparty spread (20/27 senders), but size them against a *$479k / $177k* on-chain float, not against NYSE liquidity.
*   **Play 3 — Never touch `SLV`, `META`, or `AVGO` on a volume signal.** Circularity 0.9999 / 0.9980 / 0.9925 means their tape is a closed loop. SLV's $13,981 of volume moved **$1.44** of position. The volume is real; the demand is not.
*   **Play 4 — Ignore holder count entirely on this chain.** AAPL's 10,639 holders and TSLA's 7,758 were manufactured by 3,942 zero-value transfers from 45 addresses. Holder count here measures airdrop scripts, not adoption. Use `distinctCounterparties` on *priced* transfers instead.
*   **Play 5 — Do not compute premium/discount.** `price` is a TradFi mirror; there is no independent on-chain clearing price. Any basis you compute will be **structurally zero** and will be an artifact of your own arithmetic.
